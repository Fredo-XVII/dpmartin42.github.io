About

I am currently a fourth year Ph.D candidate in the Quantitative Psychology program at the University of Virginia. I am working in the Mathematical Psychology Lab, where Timo von Oertzen is my advisor. I also spend time working in the Social Development Lab in the Curry School of Education with my secondary advisor, Sara Rimm-Kaufman, and in the Implicit Social Cognition Lab with Brian Nosek. Broadly, my interests lie in exploratory data analysis and visualization, data mining, increasing reproducibility in the scientific process (especially with respect to data analysis), and I also have a substantive interest in math engagement and achievement. If you look around, you'll find some of my current projects, publications, presentations, and relevant code.
